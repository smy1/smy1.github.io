The ABC of children

A for Acquisition

How do children learn new words? Past research focused a lot on the strategies which children employ in order to accurately map a novel word to the intended referent object. With this respect, I have examined children's reliance on word learning biases during referent selection, such as Mutual Exclusivity§ and word form-meaning systematicity§.

B for Books

What are the benefits of interactive shared book reading? Research has demonstrated that shared reading enhances children's vocabulary size because children are exposed to many new words during reading. Shared reading has also been shown to create rich parent-child interaction; hence, one of my current projects is on the link between shared reading and children's cognitive development§.

C for Curiosity

How do curiosity and interest shape learning? "Curiosity kills the cat, but satisfaction brings it back". It is not easy to distinguish between curiosity and interest, and recent studies have shown that children actively seek out information that they are interested in or are curious about. This seems intuitive enough, but researchers are still trying to unravel the mechanisms behind curiosity, interest, active learning, and children's learning outcome. In one of my on-going projects, we find that the link between active learning and learning outcome§ is not that straight-forward and may depend on the child's learning preference.

C+ for Community

How much do we know about children from the non-WEIRD population? WEIRD stands for western, educated, industralised, rich, and democratic. This is a very specific group of population and research findings based on this group may not hold true for other groups of population. Furthermore, the characteristics "educated" and "rich" (i.e., education and income level, typically used to index SES) mediate the disparity observed in children's later academic achievement. The uniqueness of different languages also means that children's word learning strategy will differ depending on what language they speak§. Given all these pressing issues, I am intrigued in conducting studies to better understand children from the non-WEIRD population.
